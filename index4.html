Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

Birth Date:October 2, 1869

Death Date: January 30, 1948


Mahatma Gandhi: The Father of the Nation

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, popularly known as Mahatma Gandhi, was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, a coastal town in present-day Gujarat, India. He was a prominent leader of India's freedom struggle against British rule and is widely recognized for his philosophy of non-violence(Ahimsa) and truth (Satyagraha). His efforts played a crucial role in securing India's independence in 1947, earning him the title "Father of the Nation."


Role in India's Independence Movement

Gandhi returned to India in 1915 and quickly became a key leader in the struggle for independence. He introduced Satyagraha and Ahimsa as the core principles of his movement. Some of his most significant campaigns included:

Champaran and Kheda Movements (1917-1918) - Fought for the rights of farmers against oppressive taxation by the British.

Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922) - Urged Indians to boycott British goods, institutions, and services.

Civil Disobedience Movement & Salt March (1930) -Led the famous Dandi March, covering 240 miles to produce salt in defiance of British laws.

Quit India Movement (1942) - Called for an immediate end to British rule, leading to mass protests and arrests.
